About DeepSight Shield

What are Deepfakes?

Deepfakes use artificial intelligence to create convincing fake videos where people say or do things they never did, posing serious risks to information integrity.

How We Detect Them

Our AI analyzes subtle inconsistencies in facial movements and lighting that humans might miss but machine learning can identify.

Accuracy Rate

Our detection system achieves over 96% accuracy in identifying manipulated videos across various quality levels and manipulation techniques.

Privacy First

Your uploaded videos are analyzed locally in your browser when possible and never stored permanently. Your privacy is our priority.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about deepfake detection and our technology.

What is this tool used for?

This tool is designed to detect whether a given video is a deepfake or a real video. Users can upload a video file or enter a video link, and the AI model will analyze it to determine its authenticity.

How does the deepfake detection work?

The system uses a machine learning model trained on real and fake videos. It analyzes video frames and patterns to detect manipulations such as face swapping, or AI-generated content.

What types of videos can I upload?

You can upload videos in common formats such as MP4, AVI, MOV, and MKV. The tool supports videos with a maximum duration of 30 seconds.

Can I check a YouTube or other online video?

Yes, if the video is publicly accessible, you can paste its URL into the provided input field, and the tool will analyze it.

How accurate is the deepfake detection?

The accuracy depends on the model, video quality, and type of deepfake. Our system provides a confidence score to indicate how certain it is about the prediction.

Does this tool store my uploaded videos?

No, we do not store or save any uploaded videos. The videos are processed in real-time, and no data is retained after analysis.

Can deepfake videos be 100% detected?

No, while our AI model is trained to detect deepfakes, some highly sophisticated deepfakes may still be difficult to identify. However, our system provides a probability score to help users make an informed judgment.

Is this tool free to use?

Yes, this tool is free for basic use. However, advanced features or extended usage may require registration in the future.

Who can use this tool?

Anyone can use this tool, including journalists, content creators, researchers, and the general public, to verify the authenticity of videos.

How can I contribute or improve the model?

If you are a researcher or developer interested in improving deepfake detection, you can contact us for collaboration opportunities.
